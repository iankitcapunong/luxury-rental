# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

High-net-worth private clients and their gatekeepers — concierges, PAs, tour managers, wedding planners, family offices — arranging chauffeured transport in central London (Mayfair, Knightsbridge, Belgravia). They arrive already expecting discretion and reliability; the site's job is to confirm, not to convince. Many visits are on the recommendation of someone who already uses the service ("by introduction only"). Mobile and desktop both matter; the mood is unhurried and evening-leaning.

## Product Purpose

A marketing site for a private chauffeur / luxury-transport company serving three London neighbourhoods only (MKB). It sells the *vision* — a journey as a curated, discreet experience — and routes serious enquiries to a concierge who replies within the hour. Success is a quiet, high-intent enquiry, not mass conversion. Credibility is carried by the testimonials/recommendations section (un-attributed, to protect clients from poaching), the fleet, and the on-board experience (champagne/wine bar, climate, vetted drivers).

## Brand Personality

Discreet, composed, quietly confident. Three words: **discreet, refined, unhurried.** Voice mottos in active use: *"Move in silence." "By introduction only." "No introduction needed."* The copy never shouts, never lists features like a spec sheet; it implies standards rather than boasting them. Emotional goal: the calm of being already looked after.

## Anti-references

- Mass ride-hail / booking-app energy (Uber, Blacklane grids of cars and prices) — too transactional.
- Loud "luxury" clichés: gold-drenched gradients, marquee-car bragging, "arrive effortlessly", "marquee cars arrive effortlessly" (explicitly rejected by the client).
- "In house" / "a household held to standard" / "private house" framing (explicitly rejected).
- Builder/tradesman driver imagery, heavy EV/"electrified" emphasis (EV is a future aspiration only).
- Anything that exposes real client identities.

## Design Principles

1. **Discretion is the product.** Restraint in palette, copy, and motion is the brand, not a limitation. When in doubt, quieter.
2. **Imply the standard; don't itemise it.** Show the cabin, the route, the calm — let credibility be inferred.
3. **Protect the client.** Testimonials and recommendations persuade without naming names.
4. **One quiet path to a person.** Every page leads, unhurriedly, to a concierge reply within the hour — two fields, not a funnel.
5. **Identity is committed.** Cormorant Garamond throughout, monochrome cream-and-black with cinematic dark sections. Polish within this; don't redesign it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body/UI text must hold ≥4.5:1 on its background (the cream `--bg` is light, so muted greys must stay dark enough). All motion — the looping scroll cue, hero video crossfade, scroll reveals, page-transition veil — must have a `prefers-reduced-motion` path. Keyboard focus must stay visible (including on the dark overlay nav). Touch targets ≥44px. Forms fully labelled.
